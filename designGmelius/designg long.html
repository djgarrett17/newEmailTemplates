How to Give Effective Feedback on Video Projects
When it comes to video feedback, quality is always better than quantity. Here are a couple of things to consider when leaving feedback for your editors:
• Consolidation is Key – When multiple team members are providing input, try to consolidate your comments before putting them in the screening room. This reduces redundancy and eliminates conflicting feedback.
• Add Clarity, Not Confusion – Be specific in your comments. Vague statements like, “I don’t love the music selection,” might be true, but it's more helpful to say, "Please replace the music track with something more uplifting." As a general rule, your comments should provide direction rather than prompt questions from the editing team.
For projects with tight deadlines, be sure to budget time for a thorough review process; otherwise, rush editing fees could apply.
